In November 2017 I gave a talk at the inaugural edition of TEDxSOAUniversity in Bhubaneswar, Odisha. The theme was *"CREATE with Butterfly Effect"* — small choices that compound into outsized outcomes. My talk was on **building better software**.

## The argument, in one paragraph

Most "better software" advice is about code — patterns, languages, frameworks. That's not where the leverage is. The leverage is in the questions you ask before you write the first line: who are you really building for, what do you refuse to ship, what compounds, what decays. The teams I've watched ship the best software weren't the ones with the cleanest architecture. They were the ones that had the most honest relationship with the problem.

>   reliability under hostile conditions.

The talk was given at SOA University, Bhubaneswar, on the chapter's maiden edition. Theme curators were big on the butterfly-effect framing — small actions, compounding outcomes — which lined up well with what I wanted to say about software being the result of thousands of small decisions, most of them invisible.
